Top 10 Things to Do in Manali for First-Time Visitors

 

Your Ultimate Manali Travel Itinerary by Adora Hospitality Solutions

 

 

Nestled in the heart of Himachal Pradesh, Manali is the crown jewel of mountain escapes in India. Whether you're traveling with family, your partner, or on a solo trip, this Himalayan wonderland offers an unforgettable mix of nature, culture, and adventure.

As a first-time visitor, here’s a curated list of the top 10 things to do in Manali, complete with local insights and stay suggestions from Adora Hospitality.

1. Explore the Boho Charm of Old Manali

Old Manali is the soul of the town—a peaceful and artistic stretch filled with:

  • Cobblestone lanes
  • Bohemian cafés
  • Tattoo parlors
  • Local bakeries
  • Live music spots

Where to stay:
Book a boutique room at Starway Homes by Adora Hospitality for an authentic Old Manali stay with modern comforts and mountain views.

2. Pay Tribute at Hadimba Devi Temple & Hadimba Park

Built in 1553, this ancient temple made of cedar-wood and stone is surrounded by tall deodar trees. It’s a place of cultural and spiritual significance.

  • Perfect for: Photography, quiet walks, and heritage lovers
  • Pro tip: Visit in the morning for peaceful vibes and better light for photos

3. Soak in Vashisht’s Natural Hot Springs

Located in Vashisht village (3 km from Mall Road), these hot springs in Manali are believed to have healing powers.

  • Entry is free, with separate bathing enclosures for men and women
  • Combine this visit with exploring nearby temples and cafés

Travel tip: After your bath, head to Vashisth waterfall for a short walk and more scenic views.

4. Take a Snowy Escape to Rohtang Pass

If you’re visiting between May and October, Rohtang Pass (13,050 ft) is a must-visit. Get ready for:

  • Snowball fights
  • Sledging
  • Snow scooter rides
  • Amazing landscape views of glaciers and peaks

Note: Permits are required to reach the place, so pre-book a taxi or rental through your hotel.

5. Paraglide Over Solang Valley

If you're an adrenaline junkie, Solang Valley is your playground. Try:

  • Paragliding: Glide over snow-covered peaks
  • Zorbing & ATV rides: Fun and thrilling!
  • Cable car ride: For panoramic views without the adventure

Best time: March to June & September to December.

6. Enjoy Manali’s Café Culture

Café hopping in Manali is about more than just food—it's a whole vibe. Top picks include:

  • The Lazy Dog (by the river)
  • Drifter’s Café (live music)
  • The Johnson’s Café (elegant dining)
  • Renaissance (classic Himachali pizzas)

Pair your meal with mountain views and a good book, or simply enjoy people-watching.

7. Take a Cultural Day Trip to Naggar Castle

A 40-minute drive from Manali, Naggar Castle is a heritage stone-and-wood structure once home to the royal family of Kullu.

  • Visit the nearby Nicholas Roerich Art Gallery 
  • Try trout fish at Naggar Castle Restaurant
  • Walk through traditional Himachali villages on your way back.

8. Shop on Mall Road

No Manali trip is complete without a shopping spree at the iconic Mall Road. Find:

  • Himachali woolens & shawls
  • Handmade soaps
  • Tibetan flags & jewelry
  • Local snacks like siddu & momos

Best time to go: Evening, when the road is bustling and lively.

9. Trek to Jogini Waterfall

A 1.5-hour scenic trek from Vashisht village leads to the majestic Jogini Falls, making it a perfect half-day adventure.

  • Ideal for beginners and couples
  • Carry snacks, water, and a camera
  • Enjoy a peaceful dip in the shallow stream beneath the waterfall

You’ll pass apple orchards, pine forests, and tiny hamlets along the way.

🏔️ Bonus: Seasonal Activities

  • Snowfall in Manali: Visit December to February for snow games, skiing, and bonfires
  • Apple blossom season: March to April
  • Trekking & camping: May to October

  • How to reach: Nearest airport: Bhuntar (50 km); Overnight buses from Delhi
  • Best time to visit: March–June for pleasant weather; December–Feb for snow experiences.
